One way to write quickly is to do as little writing as possible. Start with a rough-in: grab all of the relevant text from your inputs, and put it on or near where it needs to go in your design (Fig 2.1). If there’s one slot for a user testimonial, for instance, and you have a few to choose from, grab several that might work and drop them into that box. If you need to write labels for a tabbed navigation element, go ahead and drop in the simplest and most obvious label for each. A navigation bar that reads “Home | user stuff | promo stuff | settings and options” has you a lot further along already than “Label | label | label | label.” The sooner you move away from squiggly lines and lorem ipsum, the sooner you can get the writing done.
