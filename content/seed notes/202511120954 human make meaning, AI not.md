Con người chúng ra luôn đưa ra ý nghĩa cho khía cạnh của cuộc sống, những sự kiện vật thể, trong khi đó LLM thì không. Những cách chúng ta đưa ra ý nghĩa bao gồm:
1. Đưa ra nó tốt hay xấu như thế nào
2. Quyết định xem thứ gì đó có đáng làm hay không
3. Quyết định xem thứ này có hơn/kém thứ khác không, và hơn / kém hơn ở mức độ nào
4. Từ chối sự sự đánh giá hiện tại 3 thứ ở trên

Humans make meaning all the time — in business _and_ in life. We:
1. **Decide that something is good or bad.** For instance: “diamonds are beautiful” or “blood diamonds are morally reprehensible and I do not want anything to do with them.”
2. **Decide that something is (subjectively) worth doing or not.** “It is worth it to get a college degree.” Or “expanding into China right now is the best opportunity we have for creating long term enterprise value.”
3. **Decide that one thing is better than another, or how much they can be compared on the same scale.** “I think _Jiro Dreams of Sushi_ is a better film about ambition than _Whiplash_.” Or “I think Singapore is a better city to live in than New York” or “I don’t think you can compare Singapore with New York because one is a city state and the other is just a city, but if you must, I think Singapore is better than New York.”
4. **Reject existing judgments about the value of things, if they are worth doing, how they should be ranked, and whether their values can be compared on the same scale.** “I thought dating him was worth it but I no longer believe this” and “I disagree with society about their broad acceptance of a thing, and instead believe in the opposite, even though it might be politically costly for me to do so.” (E.g. fighting for the end of apartheid, or campaigning for women’s rights).
